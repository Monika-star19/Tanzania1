Most Beautiful Places To Visit In Tanzania

  • Serengeti National Park
    Serengeti National Park, located in Tanzania, is one of the most renowned wildlife sanctuaries in the world.
    The park is famous for its annual Great Migration, where over a million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles
    traverse its plains in search of greener pastures.

    • Best Time Visit:
    June to October and December to March
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $82.60 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $23.60 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day


  • Ngorongoro Crater
    The Ngorongoro Crater, located in Tanzania, is the world's largest inactive, intact, and unfilled volcanic caldera.
    This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including the Big Five (lion, leopard,
    elephant, buffalo, and rhino). The crater's lush vegetation and permanent water sources support a thriving
    ecosystem, making it a prime location for wildlife viewing.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and November to December
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $70.80 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $20.00 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $8.26 per adult per day


  • Mount Kilimanjaro
    Mount Kilimanjaro, located in Tanzania, is the highest peak in Africa, standing at 5,895 meters (19,341 feet) above
    sea level. This iconic stratovolcano consists of three cones: Kibo, Mawenzi, and Shira, with Kibo being the highest.
    Known as the "Roof of Africa," Kilimanjaro is renowned for its diverse ecosystems, ranging from lush rainforests
    at its base to arctic conditions at its summit.
    Best Time Visit:
    January to March and June to October
    Ticket Price
  • Park Entry Fees: $70 per person per day
  • Camping Fees: $50 per person per night (for those camping)
  • Hut Fees: $60 per person per night (for those staying in huts on the Marangu Route)
  • Rescue Fees: $20 per person per trip
  • Guide Fees: Typically around $20-$30 per day (per guide)


  • Zanzibar
    Zanzibar Island, is home to Stone Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its winding alleys, bustling bazaars,
    and historic buildings that reflect a blend of African, Arab, Indian, and European influences. Zanzibar is also renowned
    for its spice farms, offering visitors a chance to learn about and experience the island's long-standing spice trade.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and December to February


  • Tarangire National Park
    Tarangire National Park, located in northern Tanzania, is renowned for its large elephant herds, ancient baobab trees,
    and diverse wildlife.The park is bisected by the Tarangire River, which serves as a vital water source for animals during
    the dry season. Besides elephants, visitors can spot lions, leopards, cheetahs, zebras, giraffes, and various bird species.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and November to May
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $59 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $17.70 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day


  • Lake Manyara National Park
    The park is renowned for its scenic beauty, encompassing a variety of ecosystems such as groundwater forests,
    grassy plains, and alkaline soda lakes. It is particularly famous for its tree-climbing lions and large flocks of flamingos
    that inhabit the lake.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and November to May
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $53.10 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $15.80 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day


  • Ruaha National Park
    The park is named after the Great Ruaha River, which flows along its southeastern margin. Known for its rugged and
    diverse landscapes, Ruaha offers a mix of savannah, woodlands, and riverine forests. It is a wildlife haven, home to
    large populations of elephants, lions, leopards, cheetahs, and African wild dogs.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and November to May
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $59 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $17.70 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day


  • Selous Game Reserve
    The reserve is named after the famous British explorer and hunter Frederick Selous. Selous is known for its diverse
    habitats, including riverine forests, open grasslands, and swamps. The Rufiji River, which meanders through the reserve,
    provides a lifeline for the area's rich wildlife.The reserve also offers unique safari experiences such as boat safaris, walking
    safaris, and traditional game drives.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and November to March
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $59 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $17.70 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day


  • Mafia Island
    Mafia Island is a peaceful and relatively undeveloped island located off the coast of mainland Tanzania, in the Indian Ocean.
    It is part of the Mafia Archipelago, which includes several smaller islands. Known for its pristine white-sand beaches,
    clear turquoise waters, and vibrant coral reefs, Mafia Island is a haven for snorkelers, divers, and beach enthusiasts.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and December to March
    Ticket Price


  • Arusha National Park
    Arusha National Park, located in northern Tanzania, is a compact but diverse park covering approximately 137 square
    kilometers. It is known for its varied landscapes, ranging from Mount Meru, the second-highest peak in Tanzania,
    to lush rainforests, savannah plains, and alkaline lakes. The park offers breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro on clear
    days and is home to a rich diversity of wildlife, including giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, and various antelope species.
    Best Time Visit:
    June to October and December to February
    Ticket Price
  • Non-East African Residents: $53.10 per adult per day
  • Children (5-15 years): $15.80 per day
  • East African Residents: Approximately $11.80 per adult per day
